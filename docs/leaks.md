# Water Leak Detection

Seven leak sensors are installed in wet areas throughout the house. They sit flat on the floor under and around plumbing fixtures and activate the moment they touch standing water.

---

## Where the Sensors Are

| Location |
|---|
| Kitchen — under the sink |
| Laundry room — near the washing machine |
| Master bathroom — left sink |
| Master bathroom — right sink |
| Powder room |
| Office — under the sink |
| Bedroom 3 — under the sink |

Two additional sensors are on hand and can be added to any location.

---

## What Happens When a Sensor Detects Water

**Both phones receive an emergency alert immediately.** The notification:

- Bypasses silent mode and Do Not Disturb — it will sound even if your phone is silenced
- Names the exact room ("Water detected — Kitchen sink")
- Appears as a persistent alert until dismissed

This is treated as an emergency because a leak that goes unnoticed for hours can cause major damage. The alert is designed to be impossible to sleep through.

---

## What to Do

1. **Go check the room named in the alert** — don't wait
2. Look for an active drip, overflowing appliance, or standing water
3. Turn off the water supply to that fixture if needed (shutoff valve under the sink, or main shutoff)
4. If it's a false alarm (condensation, spilled water), dry the sensor off — it will clear automatically once dry

The sensors reset automatically once they're no longer in contact with water. You don't need to do anything to re-arm them.

---

## Sensor Notes

The sensors report their status every minute as part of normal operation — this is visible in the system logs but doesn't trigger any notifications unless water is actually detected. This is normal behavior and can be ignored.
